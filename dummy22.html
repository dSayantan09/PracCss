Republic of India

Location: India is located in South Asia and is the seventh-largest country by land area. It is bordered by Pakistan to the northwest, China and Nepal to the north, Bhutan to the northeast, and Bangladesh and Myanmar to the east. It also shares maritime boundaries with Sri Lanka and the Maldives. Capital: The capital city of India is New Delhi. Government: India is a federal parliamentary democratic republic. It follows a multi-party system, with the President of India serving as the head of state and the Prime Minister as the head of government. Population: India is the second-most populous country in the world, with a population of over 1.3 billion people as of my knowledge cutoff in September 2021. It has a diverse population with various ethnic, linguistic, and cultural groups. Official Languages: India has two official languages at the national level: Hindi and English. However, the country recognizes 22 officially recognized languages, including Bengali, Telugu, Marathi, Tamil, Urdu, Gujarati, Malayalam, Kannada, Oriya, Punjabi, Assamese, Maithili, and others. Economy:India has one of the world's fastest-growing major economies. It is classified as a developing country and has a mixed economy. The country has various industries, including information technology, textiles, telecommunications, pharmaceuticals, automotive, and more. Agriculture also plays a significant role in the Indian economy. Culture: India is known for its rich and diverse culture, which has been shaped by its long history, diverse religious traditions (including Hinduism, Islam, Sikhism, Christianity, Buddhism, and others), and regional variations. Indian cuisine, music, dance forms (such as Bharatanatyam, Kathak, and Odissi), traditional clothing (such as sarees and dhotis), festivals (such as Diwali, Holi, Eid, and Christmas), and art forms (such as painting and sculpture) are all significant aspects of the country's cultural heritage. Landmarks: India is home to numerous iconic landmarks and historical sites, including the Taj Mahal in Agra, Jaipur's Hawa Mahal, the Red Fort and Qutub Minar in Delhi, the Gateway of India in Mumbai, the temples of Varanasi, the backwaters of Kerala, the hill stations of Shimla and Darjeeling, and many more.
